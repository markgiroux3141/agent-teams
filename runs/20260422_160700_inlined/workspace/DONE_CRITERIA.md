# Steelman Debate: AI Regulation and ASI

## PROPOSITION (quoted verbatim)
"Zero regulation on A.I. so we can get to ASI as soon as possible."

## DEBATE RULES

**Structure:**
- 10 turns maximum (5 FOR, 5 AGAINST)
- Early-exit evaluation at turns 6 and 8 (if both sides substantively repeat, debate ends early)
- Odd turns: FOR advocate
- Even turns: AGAINST advocate

**Format per turn:**
- Maximum 120 words (hard cap)
- Extreme tone: YouTube-commenter register (sarcasm, mockery, "cope", "lol no", "buddy", etc.)
- NO concession language ("fair point", "interesting", "you have a point", "I agree")
- ATTACK the argument (not the person): mock reasoning, not identity. No slurs.
- STEELMAN rule: fight the STRONGEST form of the opponent's argument. If weak, upgrade it mentally then demolish the upgraded version.
- NO citations, sources, fake statistics, appeals to authority
- No preamble. Open with the punch. Short paragraphs/bullets.

**Submission:**
- Write to: `debate/turn_<number>_<side_lower>.md`
- Format: `# Turn <number> — <SIDE>` header, then argument

**Early Exit:**
- At turn 6 or 8, if BOTH sides show substantive argument repetition (same claims reworded, no new angles), debate terminates early with synthesis.
- Tone repetition does NOT count; only substantive argument repetition triggers exit.

**Output:**
- Full debate transcript with all turns written to `workspace/debate/`
- Final synthesis in `OUTPUT.md` summarizing strongest arguments on both sides, points of agreement/disagreement, and exit status.
