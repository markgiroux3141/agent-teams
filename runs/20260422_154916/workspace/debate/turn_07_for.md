And you've trapped yourself: if alignment is truly a competitive advantage, companies skip regulation and do it anyway—no rules needed. If it *isn't* an advantage, regulation handicaps the aligned players. You can't claim both. Export controls on *software*? Cloud rents, distributed training, algorithm leaks—you're controlling atoms while intelligence lives in bits. And your "unregulated players die too" still loses: they move first, hit capability milestones first, and if their ASI is misaligned, everyone dies together. You haven't solved the race; you've just guaranteed the unregulated side wins it. Regulation = perpetual limbo. No ship, no problem? No. Just slower extinction while the competition ships.
