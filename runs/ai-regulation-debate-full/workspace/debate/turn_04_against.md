"Business-ending liability" only works if the disaster happens *after* deployment and is *caught*. If you deploy an ASI you didn't fully verify—say, for market advantage—and it goes wrong, detection is too late. The liability never gets realized. Prisoners' dilemma, not safety net. Pharma still applies: companies have liability incentives *and still cut corners*, so regulators set pre-market testing requirements. Same fix here: gate deployment. Regulators don't need to "solve alignment"—they need to enforce "don't ship unverified superintelligence." You're conflating "unsolved science" with "no governance possible." Wrong. Standards exist for hard problems everywhere. And regulators hire technical experts; they're not committees of philosophers. The bottleneck isn't regulation preventing alignment research—it's preventing *premature deployment* of misaligned systems.
